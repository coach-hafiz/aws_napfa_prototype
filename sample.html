1
Mindset shift Early on, some students focused only on time and speed. Later, more students started saying things like “I found a rhythm” and “I felt more relaxed”.
2
Social awareness Students noticed differences in rhythm, recognised effort, and began encouraging their partners more often.
3
Running as self-discovery Many students are beginning to understand what their body tells them during a run.
1) What did you notice about your rhythm? Did it feel smooth, steady, rushed, or tiring?
2) What did your body tell you? Think about breathing, steps, flow, and fatigue.
3) What did you notice about your partner? What effort, challenge, or improvement did you see?
4) What encouragement can you give? Say something supportive, specific, and kind.
5) Pick one next-step goal Smoother rhythm • calmer breathing • steadier pace • support a partner better
